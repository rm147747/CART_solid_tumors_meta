We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Chimeric Antigen Receptor T-Cell Therapy for Solid Tumors: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Clinical Efficacy Across Target Antigens" for consideration for publication in Medical Oncology as an original research article (systematic review and meta-analysis).

This work represents the first comprehensive quantitative synthesis of CAR-T cell therapy efficacy across all solid tumor types, encompassing 13 peer-reviewed phase I/II trials, 201 patients, and 10 distinct target antigens. Using rigorous random-effects meta-analytic methods (logit transformation with REML estimation and Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman confidence interval adjustment), we demonstrate a pooled objective response rate of 19.8% with substantial heterogeneity across targets (I-squared = 52.9%, prediction interval: 3.3-64.1%). Our key finding is that target antigen selection, rather than engineering modifications, may be the primary determinant of clinical outcomes in solid tumor CAR-T therapy. Notably, CLDN18.2-targeted CAR-T in gastric cancer achieved the highest response rate (48.6%), a finding recently validated by the landmark CT041-ST-01 randomized phase 2 trial published in The Lancet (HR 0.37 for progression-free survival).

We believe this manuscript is particularly suitable for Medical Oncology for the following reasons:

1. **Scope alignment**: Medical Oncology publishes systematic reviews and meta-analyses that advance the understanding of cancer treatment. Our work provides the first quantitative benchmark for CAR-T efficacy in solid tumors — a rapidly evolving and clinically important area.

2. **Methodological rigor**: We followed PRISMA 2020 guidelines (registration: PROSPERO CRD420261294180), applied state-of-the-art statistical methods for meta-analysis of proportions, assessed certainty of evidence using GRADE, and report prediction intervals alongside conventional confidence intervals.

3. **Clinical relevance**: With six approved CAR-T products for hematological malignancies and extensive ongoing development for solid tumors, clinicians and researchers need evidence-based guidance on which targets show the most promise. Our findings directly inform this decision-making.

4. **Timeliness**: The recent publication of CT041-ST-01 — the first randomized trial of CAR-T in solid tumors — provides timely validation of our single-arm findings for CLDN18.2, making this a particularly relevant moment for a comprehensive synthesis.
